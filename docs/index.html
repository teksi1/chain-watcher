CHAIN WATCHER GUIDE

What is this for?

What is a chain?

A chain is a run of successful attacks made by faction members. The first 10 hits must be completed during the five-minute warm-up. After that, every successful hit resets the five-minute timer. If the timer reaches zero, the chain ends and goes into cooldown.

Chains earn respect for the faction. Bigger chains also reach bonus hits at 10, 25, 50, 100, 250 and higher milestones.

Why do we need Chain Watcher?

A long chain runs through work, sleep and different time zones. One uncovered five-minute window can end the whole thing. This page gives us a simple rota: who expects to be around, who is watching the timer and where we still need cover.

The schedule is a plan, not a live promise. Torn's live status can help, but an online dot does not mean that person is watching the chain.

Schedule labels

Online
You expect to be around and able to make hits.
Watching
You are actively keeping an eye on the chain timer and are ready to save it.
DUMP
You plan to spend your stored or available energy on the chain.
Offline
You will not be available during this time.

Before your slot

  • Pick times you can realistically cover, then press Save schedule.
  • Prepare several easy targets before Chain Weekend. Keep one backup target ready for an emergency hit.
  • If you are Watching, keep Torn open and make sure the hit lands before the timer reaches zero.
  • Check Discord before taking a bonus hit or changing the plan. Follow the chain lead's calls.
  • If your plans change, update your schedule and arrange a handover in #Chaining.
